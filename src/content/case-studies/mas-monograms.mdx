## The brief

MAS Monograms is a home-based custom embroidery studio run by Mary Ann Stone in St. Matthews, South Carolina. Every item she produces is made to order: the stitch count, thread color, font style, and placement all vary by customer, and each of those variables affects the price. She had a growing customer base, strong reviews, and work worth showing. She did not have a website that could handle the ordering model.

The core problem with standard e-commerce for custom embroidery is that there is no fixed price to put in a cart. A monogram on a tote bag is not the same price as a custom applique on a jacket, and the customer does not know what they want until they see the options. A standard add-to-cart flow either undersells the work or forces Mary Ann to issue refunds when the real quote comes back different from what the product page suggested.

## The approach

We built around a different idea: educate first, quote second, collect payment third.

The site's job before the quote form is to give customers enough information to make a confident request. The Style Gallery shows 60+ real finished pieces organized by technique, so a customer can point at what they want. The Font and Lettering Guide lays out every available style with examples. The Thread Color Chart shows the full palette organized by color family. The pricing page explains the four tiers and the factors that affect final cost.

By the time a customer fills out the quote form, they already know what font they want, what color thread they are thinking about, roughly what they expect to pay, and what a realistic turnaround looks like. That changes the quoting conversation from estimation to confirmation.

Squarespace handled everything cleanly at this scale: the shop organization, the gallery, the blog, and the inquiry form. The cart and checkout feature stays active only for the Clearance section where pre-made, fixed-price items are available to ship immediately.

The full build, including the pieces above:

- Shop section organized by ten item categories, from tote bags and towels to baby items and the "Bring Your Own Item" program for customers who ship in personal fabric
- Style Gallery with 60+ real customer pieces organized by technique: monograms, logos, appliques, custom designs, pet items, seasonal work, and more
- Font and Lettering Guide showing every available style with examples so customers can point at what they want
- Thread Color Chart covering the full palette organized by color family
- Pricing page with the four service tiers and a plain-English explanation of what drives cost
- How It Works page that walks the four-step process (quote, invoice, approval, production)
- Quote request form with fields for item type, text and letters, font, thread color, placement, quantity, timeline, and an option to upload reference photos
- Clearance section with Squarespace cart and checkout for ready-to-ship items
- About page with Mary Ann's story as a maker, positioning the studio as a personal craft operation rather than a fulfillment warehouse
- Customer testimonial section

## The result

The site is built so customers arrive at the quote form already fluent in the options: they have seen the gallery, picked a font, and have a realistic sense of price before they fill it out. A request that comes in this way carries enough detail to quote against directly, which is the whole point: less email back-and-forth for Mary Ann, fewer surprises for the customer.

## Reflections

The decision to skip the cart for custom work was the right call, but it took some convincing. The instinct when building an e-commerce site is to put products in a cart. For custom embroidery, the cart would have been a liability: a price that turns out to be wrong damages trust more than asking a customer to fill out a form. The quote flow is actually the better conversion path for handmade work because it filters for customers who are genuinely ready to order and sets the right expectations before any money changes hands.
