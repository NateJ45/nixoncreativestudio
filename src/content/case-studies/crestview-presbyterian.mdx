## The brief

Crestview Presbyterian came to Nixon Creative Studio with a working but dated site that buried the two things first-time visitors actually look for: service times and what to expect on a Sunday morning. The leadership team wanted a refresh that put guests first without losing the depth that current members rely on.

## The approach

We started with a half-day workshop with the communications team and two volunteer guides who regularly hand-off to new families. The conversation surfaced what was missing: visit-planning was a thirty-second decision for most guests, but the existing site treated it as a fifteen-minute reading exercise.

From there, the strategy doc focused on two parallel paths. Guests get a fast lane to service times, a what-to-expect overview, and a one-tap visit-planning form. Members get the deeper resource library, sermon archive, and small group sign-ups, just one level down so the homepage stays calm.

We made one more call up front: photography on the site would all come from one Sunday morning shoot rather than stock imagery. The visuals had to feel like Crestview, not like a generic church template.

## What we built

- A homepage that opens with service times and a Plan Your Visit button
- A what-to-expect page written from a first-time guest's perspective
- A sermon archive that loads quickly and survives the volunteer who maintains it
- A small-group finder filtered by neighborhood and life-stage
- A one-Sunday photo shoot covering the lobby, sanctuary, kids check-in, and post-service coffee

## The result

In the first month after launch, visit-planning form submissions doubled compared to the same month the year before. The communications team also reported a measurable drop in phone calls asking for service times, which freed the front office for the questions that actually need a human.

## Reflections

The biggest lesson on this one: the site only worked because the photography matched the layout. If we had defaulted to stock imagery, the redesign would have read as "new template, same place." The one Sunday shoot was worth the extra coordination.
