## The brief

The Foundation for Reformed Theology was established in memory of Dr. John H. Leith (1919-2002), Calvin scholar and Presbyterian minister, to carry forward his work of equipping clergy and serious students of Reformed theology. The organization runs annual ministerial study seminars and maintains a growing library of theological resources for ordained ministers, seminary students, and church elders.

Their existing digital presence was not keeping pace with the depth and credibility of the work. The resource library had no clear path through it. Seminar information was scattered across pages without a coherent structure. Visually, the site did not read with the authority the organization carries in its field.

## The approach

The first decision was platform. For a content-heavy, staff-maintained site where editorial quality matters and developer availability is limited long-term, WordPress was the right call. Bricks Builder gave us design precision without locking the team into an interface that fights them during content updates. ACF handled structured data for the resource library so we could sort and filter materials by topic, date, and format.

The editorial design system was built around legibility and weight. The typographic choices borrowed from academic publishing: burgundy drop caps to open major sections, fleurons as section dividers, and duotone treatment for archival photography. The goal was a site that feels like something the Foundation would be comfortable handing to a visiting scholar without apology.

The content architecture separates the work into three areas: the seminar program (what they run, who teaches, how to apply), the resource library (searchable by topic and format), and the organizational story (history, leadership, and Dr. Leith's legacy). Staff can maintain all three from the WordPress admin without developer help after handoff.

The full build, including the pieces above:

- Seminar program pages with faculty listings, schedule details, and a multi-step application workflow
- Resource library organized by topic, format, and date with ACF-powered metadata for filtering and surface
- Editorial design system: burgundy drop caps, fleurons, and duotone archival image treatment throughout
- Organizational history section drawing on Dr. Leith's correspondence, publications, and public record
- Staff and board directory
- Donation and giving page connected to the Foundation's existing giving processor
- Contact and inquiry form
- RankMath configuration for theological search terms, calibrated to the audience rather than maximum crawl coverage

## The result

The build was finished and content-complete on staging, structured so the primary operational goal is met by design: day-to-day updates, new resources, seminar details, and staff changes all happen from the WordPress admin without developer support. For an organization without in-house technical staff, that self-sufficiency was the point, not a nice-to-have.

## Reflections

The most interesting design constraint on this project was restraint. Every material decision had to clear one test: does this read as scholarly, or does it read as trying to look scholarly? Those are different things, and the Foundation's audience notices the difference. Drop caps and fleurons work here because they come from a tradition the readers recognize as their own. The same moves in a consumer context would read as affectation. Here they read as at home.
