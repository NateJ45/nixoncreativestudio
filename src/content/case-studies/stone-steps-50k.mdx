## The brief

The Stone Steps 50K is an ultramarathon trail race through Mt. Airy Forest in Cincinnati, with over twenty years of history and a loyal running community behind it. Race director David Corfman has been running the event since the early years. The race had the community, the history, and a genuinely difficult course. What it did not have was a website that treated any of those things seriously.

The brief was to build a site that served three distinct needs: give new runners enough information to decide whether to register, give returning runners a way to check results and records, and give everyone the logistical information they need to show up on race day prepared.

## The approach

A trail race website has a different content hierarchy than most event sites. The question a new visitor asks is not "what is this?" but "how hard is this and can I finish it?" The answer lives in the course details: total elevation, loop structure, aid station locations, cutoff times. We put that information front and center alongside the registration link so the path from "I've heard of this race" to "I'm registered" is as short as possible.

For the records section, we took it seriously. Ultramarathon runners care about records in a way that road runners often do not, and a twenty-year race has genuine history worth preserving. We built a proper all-time records database with age-group breakdowns for both the 50K and 27K distances, plus top-ten leaderboards by gender. The records page is not decorative; it is something runners actually check before the race and after results post.

The weather widget was a practical addition that earns its place. Mt. Airy Forest conditions on race morning are relevant information. A runner who can check current conditions at 5 AM from the race website rather than searching for a general Cincinnati forecast has one fewer thing to think about.

Registration and results both run through RunSignUp, which is the right tool for the actual race management. The site links out cleanly rather than trying to replicate that functionality.

The full build, including the pieces above:

- Event homepage with the essential information: date, location, race distances, and prominent registration link
- Course detail pages for the 50K and 27K with elevation data, loop structure, and aid station information
- All-time records database with age-group breakdowns (seven age groups for 50K, six for 27K) and top-ten leaderboards by gender for each distance
- OpenWeatherMap weather widget showing live conditions at Mt. Airy Forest
- RunSignUp integration for registration and results, linked cleanly from the site navigation
- Google Maps embed for downtown Cincinnati and airport-area hotel options for out-of-town runners
- Contact form directed to the race director
- Sponsor section featuring Fleet Feet, Cincinnati Parks, and USATF

## The result

Runners now have one place that answers every pre-race and post-race question, from course elevation to race-morning conditions. The all-time records page gives the community a reason to come back after results post each year, which is how a once-a-year event keeps a presence beyond the single October weekend it actually runs.

## Reflections

The records database took more planning than any other feature on this site. Getting the data structure right for age-group records across two distances, with separate leaderboards for all-time performance, required thinking through the display before writing a line of code. But it was the feature the community noticed most. Runners who have been doing this race for fifteen years saw their names in a real records table for the first time, and that matters to people who have been showing up to a forest in Cincinnati every October for two decades.
