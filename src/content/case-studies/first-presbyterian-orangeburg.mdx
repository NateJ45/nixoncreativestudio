## The brief

First Presbyterian Church of Orangeburg is a Presbyterian congregation in Orangeburg, South Carolina whose tagline captures the mission plainly: "Above all to glorify Jesus Christ." The church needed a website that could serve two very different groups at the same time. Visitors showing up for the first time want to know when services are, what to expect, and how to get there. Active members want to find ministry information, give online, and book the building for events. The old digital presence did not do either job well.

The church had active ministry programming across music, children, youth, adult education, and community outreach, but none of it was organized in a way that helped someone understand what the church actually does on a weekly basis. Staff also needed something they could maintain without developer help.

## The approach

The architecture we settled on follows three audience lanes. Visitors land on clear worship information and an expectations guide before anything else. Members get a ministry section that covers every program the church runs, plus a Building section for facility reservations and a Giving section tied to OnRealm. The Updates section, which includes the blog, event calendar, and weekly prayer focus materials, serves both groups depending on what they are looking for.

We chose Wix for the platform because the church's communications team needed to own the site completely after launch. Wix's built-in CMS handles blog posts and event updates without requiring any technical knowledge, and the form builder covered the facility reservation workflow without needing third-party integration.

Sermons and service videos live on Facebook and YouTube where the church already had an established presence. The site links to those platforms rather than trying to host or embed the archive natively, which keeps the content management simple and puts the videos where members are already watching them.

## What we built

- Worship information page covering Sunday service times, the online viewing option, and what first-time visitors can expect
- Ministry section with pages for music, children, youth, adult programs, service teams, and community outreach
- Building section with a facility reservation system for organizations wanting to use the church space, including an online request form
- About section covering beliefs, a pastor's note, and the staff directory
- Updates section with blog posts, a full event calendar, and weekly prayer focus materials
- Giving page connected to OnRealm for online giving
- Contact page with office hours and location information

## The result

Staff update the blog and calendar without any developer involvement. The building reservation form replaced a phone-and-email back-and-forth that had been the previous system. Online giving through OnRealm is accessible from any page on the site without requiring a visit to a separate platform.

## Reflections

The building reservation feature turned out to be more significant than the initial brief suggested. Church buildings are used by the congregation, community organizations, and partner ministries throughout the week, and coordinating those requests by email alone creates scheduling conflicts. An online form that captures the date, space, and purpose of the request up front cut the coordination time significantly and gave the office staff a record they could reference without digging through an inbox.
